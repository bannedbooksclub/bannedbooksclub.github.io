Hello and welcome! 👋

We're a warm, friendly group that loves reading and talking about books together. Each month, we pick a book that was banned somewhere in the world — and we dig into what made it so special (and so surprising!) that someone wanted it kept secret.

📖

We Read Together

One book a month, chosen by the group. All skill levels and reading paces are welcome!

💬

We Chat & Share

Monthly meetings where everyone's voice matters. There are no wrong opinions here!

🤔

We Ask Why

Why was it banned? By whom? Were they right? The history is often the best part!

Books We've Explored 🗺️

Here are some of the fascinating books we've discussed — and why they were banned!

Charlotte's Web E.B. White · 1952

Banned because: Some thought animals shouldn't be able to talk in stories. We disagreed — and cried at the ending anyway.

Where the Wild Things Are Maurice Sendak · 1963

Banned because: A few folks worried it would give children nightmares. Most kids just loved the adventure!

The Diary of a Young Girl Anne Frank · 1947

Banned because: Some found it too sad or mature. We found it incredibly brave and important.

James and the Giant Peach Roald Dahl · 1961

Banned because: Too fantastical for some! A boy, a giant peach, and some very friendly bugs. We loved every page.

Fahrenheit 451 Ray Bradbury · 1953

Banned because: This is a book about burning books — and it was itself banned! You truly can't make it up.

The Wonderful Wizard of Oz L. Frank Baum · 1900

Banned because: Some libraries called it "ungodly." We called it a delightful adventure down a yellow brick road!
